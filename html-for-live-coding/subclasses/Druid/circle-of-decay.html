Circle Spells

At 3rd, 5th, 7th, and 9th level you gain access to the spells listed for that level in the Circle of Spores Spells table.

Once you gain access to one of these spells, you always have it prepared, and it doesn't count against the number of spells you can prepare each day. If you gain access to a spell that doesn't appear on the druid spell list, the spell is nonetheless a druid spell for you.

7th Blight, Shadow of Moil
9th Cloudkill, Negative Energy Flood

Touch of Decay

At 2nd level, when you successfully dealt damage to a creature, you can use your bonus action to siphon off their life energy and apply it to yourself. You or an ally within 60 ft of you gain temporary hit points equal to twice the spell’s level casted + your Druid level (cantrips and physical attacks count as 1st level).

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier, regaining all uses after a long rest.

Corrupted Form

Also at 2nd level, while you are in your Wild Shape form, you gain +1 to AC and deal an extra 1d4 Necrotic damage when you hit a creature for the first time in a turn. This damage increases to 1d6 at 6th level, 1d8 at 10th level and 1d10 at 14th level.

Servant of Rot

At 6th level, you can Wild Shape into an Interiant, similar to a corrupted and decaying Treeaant.

Abilities

Tainted Soil (1/day): you touch the ground where you stand and spread corruption in a 15 ft radius. Any creature within the area must succeed on a Constitution saving throw equal to your Druid spell save DC or take 6d6 Necrotic damage and is Poisoned. On a successful save, they only take half the damage. A creature that failed the initial saving throw can repeat the saving throw at the start of each its turn to be cured of the Poisoned condition.

Noxious Ward (Recharges on 5-6): when you successfully hit a target, you can force it to make a Constitution saving throw or be afflicted with a Noxious Ward. While a creature has a Noxious Ward on them, at the start of each of their turn, they take 1d4 Necrotic damage and in return, you heal for the same amount. After 5 rounds or as an action, you can trigger the Ward to explode and the target will suffer 2d8 Necrotic damage.

Tainted Summons

At 10th level, any beast or fey summoned or created by a spell that you cast gains the following benefits:

  • The creature is resistant to Necrotic and Poison damage.

  • When the creature is reduced to 0 hit points, it leaves behind a lingering essence of decay and as a reaction, you can harness that essence to either heal yourself or deal extra Necrotic damage in your next attack equal to your Wisdom modifier.

The Decay Doesn’t Stop

At 14th level, when you would be reduced to 0 hit points as a reaction, you can instead choose to drop to 1 hit point. In addition, for the next minute you gain the following benefits:

  • You become resistant to Bludgeoning, Slashing and Piercing damage.

  • You can’t be Charmed, Blinded, Frightened or Poisoned.

  • If you are reduced to 0 hit points and go unconscious during this period, each creature of your choice within 30 ft takes Necrotic damage equal to your Wisdom modifier and must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or fall Prone.
